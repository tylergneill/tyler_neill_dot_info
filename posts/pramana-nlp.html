Pramana NLP

2019

Pramana NLP is a curated corpus of Sanskrit philosophy texts, specifically designed to facilitate natural language processing research. The corpus focuses on epistemological (pramana) literature from the classical Indian philosophical traditions.

Why This Corpus?

When I started my doctoral research in Leipzig, I needed a well-curated corpus of Sanskrit philosophy texts to work with. Existing digital resources were scattered, inconsistently formatted, and often lacked the metadata needed for serious NLP work.

Pramana NLP is my model of simple but informative text digitization for facilitating NLP work. The texts are carefully segmented, consistently encoded, and documented with the information researchers need.

Open Source

Like my other work, Pramana NLP is open source and licensed under a Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 4.0 International License. You can view and download it from the GitHub repository.

For more details on the corpus and its construction, see the 2019 publication.
