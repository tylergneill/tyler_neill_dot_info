Splitter Options

May 2024

The Problem with Splitting

For many, it's hard to reliably split (or "segment") a Sanskrit sentence into its component words. This requires resolving complex linguistic issues, including sandhi, ligature-based scripts, compounding, synonymy (many words per meaning), and polysemy (many meanings per word), all basically at the same time.

Splitter System Options

Fortunately, thanks to the hard work of various teams, several Sanskrit splitter systems exist. Here are the main options you can try out online today:

  1. Sanskrit Reader Companion (The Sanskrit Heritage Site): Provides color-coded, interactive HTML with various possible word splits plus additional linguistic information.
  2. Sanskrit Sandhi and Compound Splitter (2018): Outputs one best-guess plaintext answer, available on Skrutable.
  3. Joint splitter/stemmer/tagger (2020): Deployed on the BuddhaNexus web app.
  4. ByT5-Sanskrit (2024): The newest option, available through Skrutable as the default model.

Which Should You Use?

For most users, the ByT5-Sanskrit model (2024) available through Skrutable will give the best results. It represents a significant accuracy improvement over earlier models while maintaining ease of use.
