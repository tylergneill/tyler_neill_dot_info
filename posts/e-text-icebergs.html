E-Text Icebergs, Part 1: Sanskrit Wikisource

2024

When exploring digital Sanskrit resources, it's easy to look at the obvious repositories like GRETIL and think you've seen everything. But like icebergs, there's often much more beneath the surface.

Sanskrit Wikisource

One underappreciated resource is Sanskrit Wikisource, the Sanskrit-language version of the Wikimedia Foundation's library of source texts. While not as well-organized as GRETIL for scholarly purposes, it contains a surprising amount of material.

The collection includes Vedic texts, classical literature, technical treatises, and religious texts. Some of these have been carefully proofread by dedicated volunteers; others are rougher.

Using Wikisource Texts

For research purposes, texts from Wikisource should be used with care. Check the source documentation, compare against critical editions when possible, and be prepared for occasional errors. But as a starting point or for casual reference, it's a valuable resource that shouldn't be overlooked.
